The Earth Simulator Supercomputer

The Earth Simulator was a supercomputer developed by Japan in 2002 and was designed to create a virtual twin of the planet for climate modeling and other simulations.

Purpose

The main purpose was to run realistic models and holistic simulations of Earth's climate. This included the oceans and the atmosphere with a 10 km resolution. It was used for weather forecasting, environmental change analysis, and earthquake pattern analysis.

Architecture

The original Earth Simulator was a parallel vector supercomputer with 640 processor nodes each containing 8 vector processors. Which caused it to be the fastest supercomputer of the time almost 5 times faster than the supercomputer that came before it the ASCI White.
